Everything you need to run Trustora's demo

How the parametric insurance model works, how the Rialo Playground session and devnet wallet connect, and the exact premiums and payouts for every product.

01

Overview

Trustora is a parametric insurance demo. Instead of filing a claim and waiting on an adjuster, each policy is tied to a public, checkable number - rainfall in centimeters, a flight or train delay in minutes, a courier's tracking status. Cross the agreed line, and the payout is automatic.

Eight products are covered today: Crop & Rain, Flight, Train, Bike, Car, Parcel, Luggage, and Health. Bike and car own-damage cover, parcel/luggage physical damage, and health hospitalisation claims are the exceptions - those still need a short assessed report, since they aren't pure data triggers.

Every purchase runs through one devnet wallet, modeled directly on the Rialo Playground account (playground.rialo.io/account). Connect once - with a real Playground User ID and public key, or an auto-generated demo identity if you don't have one - and that same wallet buys any product on the site.

02

Getting started

Open the wallet panel

Click Connect Wallet in the top navigation of the main app. This opens your session & custodial keypair panel.

Enter your Rialo User ID + public key

Have a Rialo Playground account? Grab your User ID and Public Key / Wallet Address from playground.rialo.io/account and paste them in. Don't have one? Leave both fields blank and a demo identity is generated for you. A display name is optional either way.

Click Connect Account

Click ⚡ Connect Account →. This mints (or attaches to) your session keypair and credits it with 1,000 USDC and 1,000 USDT - enough to try every product without touching the faucet.

Pick a product and buy

Browse any vertical, choose a tier, and click Choose plan (or use the calculator's Buy with wallet button). You'll be asked for a few trigger-tracking details first - city/country for crop, flight or train number for travel plans, age and term for health - then a confirmation animation plays and the policy appears instantly in My Policies.

The Connect Rialo panel takes a User ID and a Public Key / Wallet Address - both optional. Paste in real values from your Rialo Playground account, or leave them blank and Trustora generates a demo User ID and public key for you. Once connected, the panel is split into:

04

Buying a policy

Every "Choose plan" button and the calculator's "Buy with wallet" button run the same purchase flow:

  1. Trustora checks you're connected. If not, the wallet panel opens with a prompt to connect first.
  2. For product cards, a details step opens asking for the fields that product's trigger needs - city/country for Crop & Rain, airline + flight number + route for Flight, train number + PNR for Train, registration number + zone for Bike/Car, courier + tracking number for Parcel, baggage tag for Luggage, or age + policy term for Health. The calculator collects these same fields inline, so no extra popup is needed there.
  3. It checks your balance in the currently selected currency (USDC or USDT). If it's too low, the panel opens with a nudge toward the devnet faucet.
  4. The premium is deducted from that balance, and a Policy ID is generated.
  5. A purchase-confirmation animation plays - a drawn checkmark, the policy name, premium paid, and maximum payout.
  6. The policy is added to My Policies and to the wallet panel's recent activity, with a timestamp.
Devnet only. No real money moves at any point in this flow - balances, keypairs, and policies all live in the browser tab's memory and reset on sign-out or page reload.
05

My Policies

The My Policies tab lists every policy bought in the current session as a card, with:

  • Policy ID - a short reference code, e.g. POL-4F8K2Q.
  • Premium paid - the amount deducted, in USDC or USDT.
  • Max payout - the cap if the policy's trigger fires.
  • Purchased - local date and time of purchase.
  • Status - shown as Active for every demo policy.

If you haven't signed in, the tab prompts you to connect first. If you're signed in but haven't bought anything yet, it prompts you to browse products.

Parametric triggers

Each fully parametric product checks a public data feed against a fixed threshold. Below is the trigger definition Trustora would evaluate for a sample plan from each product family.

Crop & Rain - Standard Season
// checked against district weather station rainfall
source: "district_rainfall_station"
window: "72h"
threshold: "20cm"
payout: "$130 / acre"
Flight - Delay + Cancellation
// checked against live flight-status feed
source: "flight_status_feed"
threshold: "90min delay OR cancelled"
payout: "$120"
Parcel - Loss Cover
// checked against courier tracking status
source: "courier_tracking_status"
threshold: "marked undelivered past cutoff date"
payout: "declared value, up to $150"

Bike and car own-damage cover, parcel/luggage physical damage cover, and Health hospitalisation claims are excluded from this table on purpose - those still route to a short assessed report rather than an automatic feed check.

08

Devnet funds

  • Signing in credits your wallet with 1,000 USDC and 1,000 USDT.
  • Each faucet request adds 200 units of the chosen currency.
  • Balances, activity, and policies are held in memory for the current browser tab only - they reset on sign-out or page reload.
Not real money. USDC and USDT here are devnet play-money labels for demo purposes only, with no connection to real token contracts or balances.
09

FAQ

Do I need to file a claim?

No, for any fully parametric product - the trigger check runs automatically. Bike and car own-damage, parcel/luggage physical damage, and Health hospitalisation claims still need a short report.

Can I use the same wallet for every product?

Yes. One sign-in and one devnet keypair cover all seven verticals.

What happens if I sign out?

Your balances, activity, and My Policies list are cleared for this session, matching how ending a Rialo Playground session works.

See the full FAQ on the main app.

10

Glossary

Parametric

A policy tied to a checkable number rather than an assessor's judgment call.

Trigger

The threshold that, once crossed, releases an automatic payout.

Premium

What you pay up front to hold a policy.

Payout

The maximum amount released if the trigger fires.

Devnet

A test environment where funds and transactions carry no real-world value.

Custodial wallet

A wallet where the keypair is generated and held for you inside the session, rather than in an external wallet you control.

Policy ID

A short reference code assigned to each purchased policy, e.g. POL-4F8K2Q.
